Where is it?

  • High Concept: Your phone goes missing within the mess of your room. Hurry and find it!
  • Player Experience Goals: Players may feel relatable to this situation when they misplace their phone and the axiety of trying to find their phone before they miss a call.
  • Genre: The genre of this game is a simulation game.
  • Game play: The player can use the mouse to move objects to find their missing phone before time runs out. The score begins are 3000 and decreases until it hits 0 or the phone is found and clicked on. The remaining score is the players end score.
  • Prototype goal: The prototype uses mechanics such as item moving using a mouse and button objects.
  • Features: The score decreases as time passes. The screen on the phone is different depending on if the player wins or loses.
  • Setting/Premise: The player is the protagonist and controls the game world using a mouse. The setting is present day.
  • Story: You regret neglecting your room and it is now a mess. In addition, you accidentally drop your phone within this mess right as you were going to answer a call.
  • Target Audience: The target audience are casual gamers, people of all ages, and people who know what a cellphone is.
  • Play time: This game takes less than a minute to complete.
  • Strategies: If the player is lucky then the first area they begin to search will have the phone.
  • Prototype Assets: All the art was drawn by me except for the background which is open source material. All audio is also open source.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: For this prototype, I used the Phaser's Action on click example and Drag Event Parameters example.
